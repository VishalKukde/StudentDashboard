# Student Dashboard: Course & Lesson Architecture

This document explains the structural relationship between Courses and Lessons in the application and how they are implemented across the MERN stack.

## 1. Data Relationship (The "Seed" Data)
In our system, a **Course** is the parent container, and **Lessons** are individual units within that course.

*   **Course**: Defines a learning path (e.g., "React Foundations"). It has a title, description, and level.
*   **Lesson**: A specific topic within a course (e.g., "Lesson 1: Introduction to JSX"). Every lesson is linked to a course via a `courseId`.

---

## 2. Backend Definition

### Models (`backend/src/models/`)
*   **`Course.js`**: Defines the metadata for the learning path.
*   **`Lesson.js`**: Defines the content, duration, and order. It uses a `mongoose.Schema.Types.ObjectId` to link back to the Course.

### Controllers (`backend/src/controllers/`)
*   **`courseController.js`**: 
    *   `getCourses`: Fetches all courses and calculates the count of lessons for each.
    *   `getCourseById`: Fetches a single course along with its full list of lessons.
*   **`lessonController.js`**: 
    *   `completeLesson`: This is the bridge. It marks a lesson as "done" for a specific student and simultaneously creates an **Activity** record to track the study time.

---

## 3. Frontend Implementation (`frontend/src/pages/`)

### The Lessons Page (`LessonsPage.jsx`)
This is the primary interface where both entities come together. It is divided into two main sections:

1.  **Course Selection**:
    *   Displays a grid of all available Courses (seeded from the DB).
    *   When you click a course card, it sets the `selectedCourse` state.
2.  **Lesson Display**:
    *   Once a course is selected, the page automatically fetches all lessons for that specific course.
    *   It renders "Lesson Cards" where the user can see the title and duration.
    *   **The Action**: Clicking "Mark Completed" triggers the `completeLesson` flow mentioned above.

---

## 4. User Interaction Flow

1.  **Discovery**: Student logs in and lands on the **Dashboard** to see overall progress based on static seed data.
2.  **Selection**: Student visits the **Lessons Page** and chooses a **Course** (e.g., "MERN Project Architecture").
3.  **Engagement**: The student sees the 10 **Lessons** for that course.
4.  **Logging**: 
    *   **Automatic**: Student clicks "Mark Completed" on a lesson. The system saves the lesson's duration as a new "Activity."
    *   **Manual**: Student uses the **Activity Form** at the top. They pick the Course and Lesson from the dropdowns to log custom time.
5.  **Analytics**: The **Activity** is saved in the database, updating the "Study Trend" and "Learning Streak" on the Dashboard.

## Summary
The **Course Page** (logic inside LessonsPage) handles high-level navigation, while the **Lesson Page** (the specific list view) handles granular progress tracking. Together, they ensure that every minute a student spends learning is tracked and visualized.

---

## 5. Roles & Detailed Flows

### A. Student Flow (Learning & Logging)
1.  **Authenticated Access**: Students log in to a personalized dashboard showing their unique learning metrics.
2.  **Progress Monitoring**: They see their "Daily Learning Trend" and "Current Streak" (calculated via backend utility) to stay motivated.
3.  **Course Navigation**: They browse available courses via the **Lessons Page** and drill down into specific lesson units.
4.  **Active Learning**:
    *   **Automated Marking**: Once a lesson is finished, clicking "Mark Completed" automatically creates an **Activity** record using the lesson's predefined duration.
    *   **Manual Logging**: For more granular tracking, students use the **Activity Form** to log custom time for specific course/lesson combinations.
5.  **Evidence of Learning**: Students can export their learning logs as PDF or CSV files (via `exportController`) for proof of study.

### B. Mentor Flow (Management & Oversight)
1.  **Cohort Health**: Mentors access a high-level **Mentor Overview** showing aggregate stats like Average Completion Rate and Total Learning Hours across all students.
2.  **Performance Tracking**: 
    *   **Leaderboard**: Automatically generated list of top-performing students to encourage healthy competition.
    *   **Risk Mitigation**: The system flags "**At-Risk Students**" who have not logged any activity in the last 7 days or whose completion rate is below 20%.
3.  **Individual Drill-down**: Mentors can view the specific dashboard of any student (using `studentId` as a query parameter) to analyze their time distribution and learning consistency.
4.  **Strategic Guidance**: Mentors use the **Recommendations** generated by the system (e.g., "Needs more focus on Backend logic") to provide personalized feedback.
5.  **Data-Driven Mentoring**: Instead of guessing progress, mentors use hard data from the **Analytics Service** to drive their weekly check-ins.
